The Complete UNILAG Post-UTME Preparation Guide for 2026

20 May 2026
8 min read
Prezidox Team

The UNILAG Post-UTME screening is one of the most competitive university entrance assessments in Nigeria. With thousands of candidates sitting the exam every year, preparation quality is what separates those who gain admission from those who do not.

This guide covers everything you need — from the subject combination structure to the most effective study strategies — to give you the best possible chance in 2026.

Understanding the UNILAG Post-UTME Format

The University of Lagos Post-UTME is a Computer-Based Test (CBT) that assesses candidates across a set of subjects depending on their chosen course of study. The exam typically includes:

  • Use of English — compulsory for all candidates
  • Mathematics — compulsory for most Science and Social Science programmes
  • General Knowledge — compulsory for all candidates
  • Course-specific electives — based on your JAMB subject combination
The Post-UTME score is combined with your JAMB score to calculate your aggregate, which determines whether you gain admission.

How the Aggregate Score is Calculated

UNILAG uses a weighted combination of your JAMB score and Post-UTME score. While the exact formula is subject to change, the general approach is:

  • JAMB score: 50% weight
  • Post-UTME score: 50% weight

This means even if your JAMB score is high, a poor Post-UTME performance will significantly reduce your aggregate. Conversely, a strong Post-UTME can compensate for a moderate JAMB score.

Key Insight
The minimum cut-off score for most competitive courses (Medicine, Law, Engineering) is typically above 60 in the Post-UTME. For less competitive courses, 50 and above is often sufficient, but you should aim higher for a safety margin.

Subject-by-Subject Preparation Strategy

Use of English

This section tests comprehension, summary, lexis and structure, and oral English. Focus on:

  • Reading comprehension passages — practise active reading and identifying the main idea quickly
  • Vocabulary in context questions — learn roots, prefixes, and suffixes
  • Grammatical structures — subject-verb agreement, tenses, pronouns

Mathematics

UNILAG Post-UTME Mathematics covers core WAEC/JAMB topics. High-yield areas include:

  • Algebra — quadratic equations, simultaneous equations, progressions
  • Statistics and probability
  • Trigonometry — SOHCAHTOA, identities
  • Sets, matrices, and differentiation/integration (for Science candidates)

General Knowledge

This is often the most unpredictable section. Typical content includes:

  • Nigerian history and government
  • Contemporary affairs (current events as of the exam year)
  • Basic science and technology facts
  • Geography — physical and human geography of Nigeria and Africa

The Most Effective Study Methods

Based on performance patterns across thousands of candidates, here are the study approaches that consistently produce the best results:

  1. Timed practice over passive reading. Reading textbooks does not prepare you for exam conditions. Sit timed mock exams regularly — at least 3 per week in the final month of preparation.
  2. Past questions first. UNILAG questions follow patterns. Solving the last 5–7 years of past questions for each subject is more valuable than covering every possible topic.
  3. Identify and attack weak areas. After each practice session, note the topics where you consistently lose marks. Spend extra time on those topics specifically — not just re-reading, but actively solving more questions from them.
  4. Do not ignore General Knowledge. Many candidates focus exclusively on their course subjects and neglect GK. This is a mistake — GK questions are often straightforward if you read current events regularly.
